# Logo Prompt Compiler

Use this only after strategy, cultural/art foundation, and construction review are
complete. The dossier is for design reasoning. It is not the image prompt.

## The Separation Rule

Keep three layers separate:

1. **Evidence layer**: product facts, name history, cultural source, art theory.
2. **Design layer**: one visual thesis, silhouette character, rhythm and invariant.
3. **Construction payload**: the minimum reproducible geometry that carries the idea.
4. **Model layer**: a short art-direction prompt describing only what should be seen.

Never paste evidence essays or product workflows verbatim into the model layer.
Never delete the construction payload during compression.

## Why Long Logo Prompts Fail

Image models treat named concepts as visible objects and exact counts as diagram
instructions. Product words such as boundary, audit, rollback, policy, register,
transaction, authority, passage and record commonly collapse into gates, arrows,
cards, links, flowcharts or UI icons.

Long negative lists also activate the visual priors they name. Ban only the three
most probable collisions. Review and reject the rest after generation.

## Compile the Visual Thesis

Reduce the full dossier to these fields:

```text
Identity character: <three perceptual qualities>
Dominant silhouette: <one holistic silhouette description>
Signature event: <one visual event, no product terminology>
Form language: <curve/angle/mass/counterform behavior>
Topology: <connectedness, enclosure, crossing, nesting, or open/closed behavior>
Construction invariant: <ratio, repeat, alignment, or transformation rule>
Counterform and terminal rule: <what must remain visibly true>
Color behavior: <structural palette role>
Likely collisions: <three maximum>
```

Bad thesis:

```text
Two modules cross a capability boundary, create an audit trace, and preserve a
rollback path with one registered interval.
```

Good thesis:

```text
A compact, calm silhouette with one poised interruption; broad warm mass, narrow
retained light, and an asymmetric balance that feels deliberate rather than fast.
```

The good thesis preserves visual consequences while removing diagram nouns. It is
still incomplete until another designer could reconstruct its defining relationship
from the topology and invariant fields.

## Prompt Template

Keep the main prompt around 45–90 words:

```text
Create one standalone brand symbol, centered on a plain <background>. Its outer
silhouette is <dominant silhouette>. Construct it from <primitive relationship>
using <topology>. Preserve this invariant: <construction invariant>. Its defining
counterform or terminal is <rule>. Use <form language>. Palette: <colors and roles>.
One flat symbol only, no text, mockup, board, shading, or perspective. Avoid
<collision 1>, <collision 2>, and <collision 3>.
```

Do not include:

- the full brand story;
- buyer sentence or product workflow;
- names of benchmark companies;
- incidental exact counts; include counts when they are essential to identity;
- art-movement names when they merely cause surface imitation;
- exhaustive forbidden-object lists;
- claims such as world-class, award-winning, genius or iconic;
- contradictory demands such as minimal but complex or organic hard geometry.

## Family Variation

During variation inside one approved family, change one high-level variable per branch:

- silhouette: compact/open, vertical/horizontal, stable/poised;
- signature event: interruption, fold, compression, counterform, phase;
- form language: taut curves, cut planes, monoline skeleton, dense field;
- visual temperament: restrained, humane, institutional, editorial, technical.

Do not vary every property at once. Do not use a shared generated parent until one
family is approved.

## Family Diversity Gate

Before a multi-family batch, compare this matrix:

```text
Family | source | topology | silhouette | invariant | first collision
```

At least three columns among source, topology, silhouette, and invariant must change
between any two families. Adjectives, palette swaps, and synonymous operations do
not count. Reject batches dominated by rounded compact masses, central gaps,
symmetrical badges, app tiles, petals, shields, chat bubbles, targets, or generic
letter-like folds.

## Two-Pass Generation

1. **Silhouette proof:** one-color, flat, no effects; test topology, recall, and
   collision. This is a diagnostic proof, not the proposed client palette.
2. **Identity rendering:** only surviving families receive structural brand color,
   optical correction, and controlled variants.

Do not spend the color pass on a silhouette that has no ownable invariant.

## References

If image references are necessary, attach one craft reference that expresses the
desired proportion or tension. Do not attach competitor logos or trend boards.
State that visible geometry must not be copied.

## Evaluation

Judge the image against the dossier after generation. The model does not need to
explain the theory. A candidate passes when its visible form fulfills the theory's
consequences, another designer can state its invariant without reading the story,
and it survives collision review. "Looks premium" is not a pass condition.
