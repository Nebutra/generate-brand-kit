# Logo Cultural and Artistic Foundations

Read this before generating a graphic symbol. A symbol without a documented
cultural, historical, scientific, typographic, or artistic foundation does not
enter the image-generation DAG.

This document governs human design reasoning. Never paste its dossier, historical
explanation, product mechanism, or construction checklist directly into an image
prompt. Compile it through `logo-prompt-compiler.md` first.

## 1. Why Foundation Matters

A durable symbol is not an arbitrary abstract shape with a story attached later.
It compresses a defensible source into a repeatable construction rule. The source
may come from the name, product mechanism, local culture, a scientific model, a
craft tradition, typography, mathematics, philosophy, or art history.

The source does not need to be obvious to every viewer. It must, however:

- be true and relevant to the brand;
- constrain form before generation;
- survive without an explanatory illustration;
- produce a specific construction rather than a mood adjective;
- avoid appropriating sacred or community-owned symbols without permission;
- remain useful after the current design trend fades.

## 2. Evidence Labels

Label every interpretation:

- **Official fact**: stated in an official brand guide, press kit, product source,
  or attributable designer statement.
- **Repository evidence**: implemented product behavior or documented strategy.
- **Historical/art reference**: supported by a named movement, artifact, text, or
  established visual tradition.
- **Design inference**: a formal reading made from the visible asset; never present
  it as the brand owner's stated meaning.
- **Stakeholder hypothesis**: a direction awaiting approval.

Do not upgrade inference or hypothesis into official history.

## 3. Benchmark Studies

### OpenAI

**Official facts**

OpenAI calls its symbol the Blossom. Its official design page states that the mark
represents the intersection of humanity and technology: circles carry fluidity and
human warmth; right angles carry technological precision and structure. The
wordmark uses OpenAI Sans, with a perfect-circle O and fixed balance/proportions.
The brand guide prescribes clear space, monochrome use, and prohibits altering,
texturing, recoloring, or casually pairing the Blossom with the primary wordmark.

**Why it works**

- One philosophical duality becomes two geometric primitives.
- Repetition and rotational symmetry create a memorable whole.
- Interlocking positive/negative space makes the construction self-supporting.
- The symbol has a proper name, a stable canon, and strict usage rules.
- The wider typography system repeats the human/technical balance.

Do not copy the knot, sixfold symmetry, or interlocking topology. Learn from the
chain: philosophy -> primitive contrast -> construction -> typography -> rules.

### Anthropic

**Official facts**

Anthropic's current official press kit distributes separate Anthropic logo and
Anthropic symbol assets in Slate and Ivory, including SVG and PNG masters. The
standalone symbol is a compressed typographic construction derived from `AI`.

**Design inference**

- The mark uses the company's domain initialism rather than an illustrated AI
  metaphor.
- Extreme compression turns ordinary letters into a distinct silhouette while the
  triangular A counter preserves recognition.
- A heavy single-color form matches the company's sober, research-led voice.
- The identity gains strength from reduction and typographic authority, not from a
  decorative narrative.

Anthropic does not publicly state these inferred meanings in the retrieved press
materials. Treat them as formal analysis only.

### Perplexity

**Official facts**

Perplexity's current official favicon is a constructed single-color line symbol
distributed from `perplexity.ai/favicon.svg`. Its geometry is symmetrical and uses
one consistent stroke system.

**Design inference**

- Folded-page, open-book, compass/star, woven-grid, and P/X readings coexist inside
  one construction.
- A central crossing organizes four directional fields, suggesting intersection
  and multiple paths without drawing a search box or magnifying glass.
- The same line skeleton creates internal negative spaces and the outer silhouette.
- Its complexity remains governed by symmetry, shared nodes, repeated angles, and
  constant stroke width.

Perplexity's accessible brand page did not provide an official meaning statement.
Do not present search, citation, book, compass, or intersection readings as official.

## 4. Foundation Families

Select one primary foundation and at most one supporting foundation.

### Name and etymology

Use the real linguistic, historical, geographic, scientific, or mythic source of
the name. Verify the source and cultural context. Do not use a name's incidental
visual associations when they contradict the product.

### Product mechanism

Use an implemented relationship unique to the product. Translate relationships
into operations, not diagrams. Mechanism supplies structure; it does not excuse an
ugly or generic result.

### Scientific or mathematical model

Use a named model whose topology or transformation genuinely relates to the brand:
symmetry groups, graph structure, phase change, orbital mechanics, wave interference,
tiling, topology, or information theory. State which property is used. Do not add a
golden-ratio spiral after the fact.

### Art history and visual culture

Use a named movement or discipline for its actual formal logic:

- Constructivism: structural tension, geometry, material and social purpose;
- Bauhaus: integration of art, craft, function and reproducible form;
- De Stijl: reduction, orthogonal relationship and controlled color fields;
- Op Art: perceptual interaction, figure-ground instability and rhythm;
- Minimalism: material presence, repetition and removal of incidental expression;
- Japanese mon: compact silhouette, radial order, inheritance and reproducibility;
- seal carving: compression, positive/negative cutting and controlled irregularity;
- calligraphy: stroke order, pressure, rhythm and terminal character;
- astronomical diagrams or scientific instruments: observation, registration,
  measurement and orientation, not generic stars or planets.

Do not imitate the surface appearance of a movement. Extract one documented formal
principle and connect it to product or name evidence.

### Craft and material tradition

Joinery, weaving, folding, engraving, typesetting, instrument making, cartography,
or bookbinding can supply operations and tolerances. Specify the real technique;
avoid generic words such as crafted, organic, premium, or timeless.

### Typographic foundation

A monogram or symbol may arise from initials, ligatures, counters, punctuation,
phonetics, or writing systems. Preserve name recognition and do not invent false
etymology. Confirm multilingual implications.

## 5. Required Concept Dossier

Create this dossier before any graphic-symbol prompt:

```text
Brand:
Buyer sentence:
Primary source/typology:
Evidence label and citation:
Why this source belongs to the brand:
Named artistic/scientific/craft principle:
Formal primitives:
Construction operation:
Positive/negative-space rule:
Symmetry or asymmetry rule:
Stroke/corner/terminal rule:
Color's structural role:
Small-size simplification:
Cultural or legal risks:
Nearest category collisions:
One-sentence symbol meaning:
```

A dossier fails when it contains only adjectives, generic business values, or a
story that does not constrain geometry.

## 6. Construction Canon

Before generation, state measurable invariants:

- primitive count and type;
- module or stroke relationship;
- angle family;
- repeat count;
- symmetry order or deliberate asymmetry;
- counterform width;
- terminal behavior;
- minimum surviving gap;
- permitted color count;
- clear-space unit;
- micro-mark deletion rule.

The canon is not a decorative golden-ratio overlay. It is a reproducible set of
relationships that another designer can rebuild.

## 7. Rejection Rules

Reject a graphic-symbol direction before generation when:

- no named source or theory supports it;
- the source was selected only because its pictures look fashionable;
- the explanation was written after seeing the generated shape;
- the form could represent any AI company unchanged;
- a sacred, national, indigenous, or community symbol is used without context and
  authorization;
- the construction depends on gradients, shadows, or presentation scale;
- it copies the topology, symmetry, silhouette, or key counterform of a benchmark;
- it cannot be reconstructed from the dossier;
- it has no rule for 16–24 px survival.
