# Logo Skin Systems

Use a Logo Skin system when an approved identity must adapt across repeated
campaigns, product states, communities, materials, or cultural contexts without
redrawing the core mark.

## Asset classes

Keep these classes separate:

1. **Canonical identity:** approved symbol and wordmark geometry.
2. **Skin master:** one isolated symbol or wordmark with one variation mechanism.
3. **Lockup:** approved symbol and approved wordmark combined after independent review.
4. **Application:** a flat composition for a real surface or channel.
5. **Mockup:** evidence that an approved application works in context.

Generate and approve symbol skins separately from wordmark variants. Combine
them only after both masters are stable; this reduces image-model workload and
allows reliable recombination.

## Configurable family profile

Select only families justified by the product and its consumption surfaces.
Useful starting dimensions include:

- canonical and accessibility-safe variants;
- evergreen color, print, and material variants;
- computational, data, developer, or runtime-state variants;
- art-historical, cultural, and editorial variants;
- seasonal, community, partnership, and campaign variants;
- experimental motion, spatial, or future-material variants.

These are prompts for scope design, not mandatory levels. Do not hard-code the
family names, number of skins, style references, colors, angles, dimensions, or
model-specific resolution into the skill.

## Dependency pattern

For each selected family:

1. Use the approved symbol or wordmark as the canonical geometry reference.
2. Create a neutral family anchor only when several children need the same
   camera, composition, material depth, or rendering language.
3. Generate formal children with explicit canonical and anchor roles.
4. Review masters for identity fidelity, family consistency, and sibling
   differentiation.
5. Promote accepted masters, then branch into lockups, applications, and
   mockups using those exact outputs as references.

Anchors and rejected candidates are dependencies or explorations, not
deliverables. A large asset count is valid only when the selected families and
applications map to real use cases; do not inflate scope with near-duplicates.

## Acceptance

- Preserve the canonical silhouette, counterform, proportion, and orientation
  unless the approved responsive-identity rules explicitly allow a change.
- Give each skin one observable mechanism and a named usage context.
- Keep native ratio and dimensions consistent within each master class.
- Verify light/dark behavior and minimum-size recognition where the skin will
  be used as an identity rather than illustration.
- Record dependencies, status, dimensions, QA evidence, prompt/input versions,
  retries, and cost in the brand asset manifest.
