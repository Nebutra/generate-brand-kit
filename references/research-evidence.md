# Research Evidence Contract

Use this contract whenever external claims influence strategy, differentiation,
trends, accessibility, platform behavior, rights, or production decisions.

## Evidence Levels

1. **Supplied fact:** stated by the user or an authoritative project document.
2. **Repository evidence:** observed directly in source, product behavior, analytics,
   assets, configuration, or tests.
3. **Primary external source:** standard, regulator, platform owner, original
   research, author/designer, official case study, or rights holder.
4. **Secondary synthesis:** credible publication summarizing primary material.
5. **Hypothesis:** a plausible interpretation that still needs validation.
6. **Aesthetic reference:** useful visual precedent, never market evidence.

Label hypotheses and aesthetic references explicitly. Do not promote them to
research findings because several examples look similar.

## Source Discipline

- Prefer primary sources for standards, platform requirements, quantitative
  claims, accessibility thresholds, legal/rights facts, and named methodologies.
- Use secondary sources for discovery or accessible summaries; retain the
  primary citation when one exists.
- Attach a direct URL, publisher/author, publication date, and verification date
  to time-sensitive claims. Recheck trend and platform material before use when
  the verification date is older than 12 months.
- Do not include a percentage, ranking, market-value impact, or adoption count
  without a direct source supporting that exact number and scope.
- Distinguish industry consensus from one studio's trend report. Consensus needs
  converging evidence across independent sources or a durable standard/canon.
- Record conflicts instead of averaging them into a false certainty.

## Project Output

Keep a compact evidence register with: claim, level, source, date, relevance,
owner, confidence, and design consequence. Strategy documents may summarize the
consequence, but must preserve a path back to the evidence register.
